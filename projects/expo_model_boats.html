Overview

The purpose of this project was to design and fabricate small-scale demonstration models that reflect the basic architecture of a solar-powered hydrofoil racing boat — including the hull, deck, struts with foils, propulsion system, and photovoltaic panel. These models were developed as part of the AGH Solar Boat initiative for use at the Osaka Expo 2025, as well as for showcases, presentations, and workshops.

The main educational goal was to demonstrate the conversion of solar energy into mechanical motion by driving a small propeller mounted on a small DC motor directly from a solar panel. The models were designed to be visually appealing, mechanically simple, and easy to assemble or mass-produce for hands-on demonstrations. They also serve as meaningful gifts for sponsors and partners.

An extended version of the model included onboard electronics with an ESP32 Wi-Fi microcontroller and a custom PCB, enabling remote steering via a web interface. These interactive boats were capable of operation in small containers, making them ideal demonstrators for exhibition environments.

Technical Highlights

  • What you did / implemented
    • Proposed and initiated the project based on Osaka Expo 2025 workshop goals set by the AGH Solar Boat supervisor.
    • Designed a 3D-printable boat model sized to match available PV panels and miniature coreless DC motors.
    • Validated that the PV panel could directly power the motor under strong lighting.
    • Created a parametric CAD model and refined it through multiple 3D-printed prototypes for proper fit and performance.
    • Built several units of the passive PV-powered demonstrator.
    • Selected electronics (ESP32, H-bridge, servo, Li-Ion battery) for a motorized RC-capable version.
    • Developed a PCB in EasyEDA, starting with a perfboard prototype.
    • Integrated INA229 current/voltage sensors, SSD1306 OLED display, battery protection, and a basic PV DC-DC converter.
    • Wrote firmware and a custom web interface for Wi-Fi steering via the ESP32.
    • Redesigned the hull to house a steering mechanism with bearing, Li-Ion cell, servo, and full PCB integration.
    • Conducted functional tests in a water tank during autonomous boat trials.
  • Interesting challenge
    • PCB layout constraints: fitting full monitoring and control circuitry into a compact board for a tiny hull space.
    • Mechanical packaging: integrating the rudder linkage and bearings within the model’s limited internal volume.
    • Firmware/UI: designing a responsive, minimalist Wi-Fi interface on a low-resource embedded system.
  • Technologies used
    • 3D Printing (FDM)
    • Fusion 360 (CAD modeling)
    • EasyEDA (PCB design)
    • ESP32 with ESP-IDF and Arduino framework
    • I2C peripherals: INA229, SSD1306
    • DC-DC converter, Li-Ion battery protection circuits
    • Wi-Fi control interface (HTML + JS)
    • Perfboard prototyping and soldering

Results

The passive models reliably demonstrated PV-to-mechanical energy conversion and were successfully used in outreach events and presentations. The Wi-Fi-controlled version performed well in live demos, operated stably in small test tanks, and provided hands-on interactivity during workshops. Limitations included limited power output under indoor lighting and tight space constraints that required careful tuning of every subsystem.
